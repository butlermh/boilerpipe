Arnold Schwarzenegger in The Last Stand

By  Cindy Pearlman

New York Times Syndicate Sunday January 13, 2013 10:11 AM

  • Comments: 0
  • Tweet

He said he would be back.

Arnold Schwarzenegger, that is. The actor turned California governor turned actor is starring in The Last Stand.

In 2003, when he put his career on hold to enter politics, Schwarzenegger was 55, an aging action star who was one of the biggest names in Hollywood. Today, he is 65, eligible for Medicare and one of the biggest question marks in town. Is he still a movie star, after a decade in which he filmed nothing but a couple of cameos in other people’s movies, or is The Last Stand, well, his last stand?

The past couple of years have been hard for Schwarzenegger. Term-limited out of the governorship and unable, thanks to his Austrian birth, to make the run for president that many say he would have liked to tackle, he instead spent much of the past year in the tabloid headlines. His marriage to newswoman Maria Shriver broke up upon her discovery of a long-ago affair between him and a former housekeeper, which had produced a son. If recent years have been hard on Schwarzenegger, it doesn’t show.

“I just got back from a one-week ski trip with my kids in Sun Valley,” he says in his still-thick Austrian accent. “Of course, kids try to out-ski you. It becomes a fierce competition. I can still do all that stuff. I feel great physically.”

So what is it like for a former Mr. Universe to turn 65?

“I can’t tell you about aging except that it sucks,” Schwarzenegger says. “I’m no different than you. We all look in the mirror and say, ‘What happened?’ You once had muscles and now, slowly, they’re deteriorating. If you do work out, you stay in shape and feel good. I feel good right now.”

The Last Stand casts Schwarzenegger as the sheriff of a small Arizona town who goes up against a vicious drug cartel. It has all the fisticuffs, gunplay and explosions that made him a worldwide box-office champion in the 1980s and ’90s.

It might be his comeback film, the actor says, but it shouldn’t be a surprise to anyone that he is back.

“It’s great to be back in a starring role,” he says. “As you remember, when I got into the governorship in 2003, I said, ‘I will go in and run the state for seven years. Then I will be back in the movie business.’ “I was a public servant for seven years,” he says. “Now I’m back to my regular life again.”

It remains to be seen, of course, whether his fan base is still there.

He eased back into show business with the help of his friend, Sylvester Stallone, who cast him in cameo roles in the surprise hit The Expendables (2010) and its 2012 sequel. He hasn’t starred in a movie since Terminator 3: Rise of the Machines (2003).

“This movie was a perfect test for me,” Schwarzenegger says, “because it required a lot of stunts and a lot of action and physical work. The director was a fanatic about seeing as much of me as possible. Only if I could have gotten killed, the stunt guys took over.”

The hot rumor has Schwarzenegger lined up for a new Terminator movie. He says that it isn’t true — yet.

“I love that character,” he says with a grin. “Give me a great script.”

  • From the Gym

    Exercising while pregnant

    My niece-in-law recently found out she is pregnant with her second. Not long ago, she had asked for my help setting up an exercise program to help her lose weight from her first (not even one year old yet!). She informed me she was pregnant during the holidays by telling me how she started to do my exercise routines, but then had to stop because, well, she was pregnant.

    Congratulations! But ... why can't you exercise?

    I can't say this loud enough: Do not use being pregnant as an excuse to be sedentary and eat crap.

    I did that with my fourth child four years ago and it's taken this long to undo the damage. Working out used to be a routine for me. In fact, when I was in my 20s I was very much into bodybuilding. With all three of my older kids, I continued to work out - including lifting weights - throughout pregnancy. With my last, however, I simply stopped. Plus, I ate the grossest stuff. It makes me nauseous to think about it now. So don't do that.

    Check with your doctor first, of course, but there is no reason you cannot work out while pregnant. Sure, there are certain things you shouldn't do - such as anything involving lying on your back - but if you're perfectly healthy and it isn't considered a high-risk pregnancy, keep doing what you're doing ... unless it's being sedentary; then I suggest starting with brisk walking.

    Osama's been gotten

    Just about everybody knows how this story goes, but they still won't want to miss a second of this dramatic tale.

    Moral dilemmas not withstanding, Zero Dark Thirty tells the story of how public enemy No. 1 Osama bin Laden was killed by military special forces. Kathryn Bigelow and Mark Boal, the team that created The Hurt Locker, also put together Zero Dark Thirty.

    The movie has been highly touted, receiving Academy awards nominations for best picture and best actress.

    Despite the criticism the movie has received for its depiction of torture, Bigelow and Boal created a film that keeps viewers captivated while allowing them to debate Zero Dark Thirty's moral issues as an outside observer.

    "The final attack, perfectly staged and executed, underscores the filmmakers’ resolve to tell the gripping story in something as close to balanced objectivity as possible," Dispatch critic Frank Gabrenya said in his review.

    He rated the movie four stars.

    Oscars: 'Lincoln' leads the way

    Like most historical analyses of the Presidency, Lincoln has come out on top.

    Steven Spielberg's Civil War drama received 12 Academy Awards nominations, including best picture, best actor, best actress and best director. Other movies that reaped nominations in multiple categories included Life of Pi, Django Unchained and Les Miserables.

    Some famous names were left off the nomination list. Kathryn Bigelow, director of Zero Dark Thirty, was snubbed even though her film was nominated for best picture. Critically acclaimed movies like Moonrise Kingdom and The Master were left off the best picture list completely.

    The winners will be announced at the ceremony Feb. 24.
