 Rising expenses are putting pressure on credit-card issuers, as the benefits from improving borrower behavior wanes and loan-portfolio growth prompts some lenders to set aside more money for future losses.

American Express Co. and Capital One Financial Corp., two of the industry's biggest companies, each said Thursday their provision for loan losses and expenses increased in the fourth quarter, weighing on earnings.

Capital One's provision jumped 33.7% to $1.2 billion from a year earlier due in part to its acquisition last year of HSBC Holdings PLC's U.S. credit-card business, through which it added about $30 billion in loans.
