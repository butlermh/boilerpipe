 The shine is coming off sterling, as investors dump the currency amid worries the U.K. economy could head back into recession.

The U.K. currency, also called the British pound, has weakened 1.5% against the dollar this year, the biggest decline of any major currency after the yen and Swiss franc.

To rev up growth and fend off its third recession in five years, the Bank of England is likely to resume its bond-buying program, investors said. This would increase the supply of sterling in the economy, thus depreciating its value and pushing the pound lower.
